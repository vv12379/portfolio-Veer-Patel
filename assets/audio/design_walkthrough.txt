This is a walkthrough of my PCB design workflow for a dual-channel active low-pass filter. I started in KiCad's schematic editor by placing two LM741 op-amp symbols and configuring the feedback networks with 10 kilohm resistors and 100 nanofarad capacitors to set the cutoff frequency at approximately 160 hertz. After running the electrical rules check and resolving all warnings, I assigned footprints — SOIC-8 packages for the op-amps and 0603 SMD pads for all passives. The netlist was then exported to KiCad's PCB editor, where I defined a two-layer board outline, placed components with a focus on signal flow, and routed all traces manually using 0.25 millimeter widths for signal lines and 0.5 millimeter for power rails. I ran the design rules check, generated Gerber files, and exported the bill of materials for fabrication review.
